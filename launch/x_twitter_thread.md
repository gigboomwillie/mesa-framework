# X/Twitter Thread: MESA Origin Story
## By Phillip Austin

---

## HOOK TWEET

I spent three months watching AI agents break my company in creative ways. Then I stopped trying to prevent failure and started building institutional memory into governance files instead. This is the story of MESA — and why every agent-driven company needs something like it.

---

## TWEET 2

A year ago I was a solo founder in central Oregon trying to run a real SaaS product on 11 AI agents. No playbook existed. No framework. No "here's what to do when agents hallucinate." So I became the experiment. GigBoom needed to work. The agents needed to get better. Fast.

---

## TWEET 3

The first three months were brutal. Agents would contradict each other. They'd drift from decisions. They'd forget context. They'd optimize for the wrong thing. And the worst part? I'd fix the same problem five times before realizing it was a pattern, not an incident.

---

## TWEET 4

That's when it clicked: agents carry nothing between sessions. They read everything they know from files at startup. Your governance files aren't documentation. They're your company's brain. Every correction makes every agent smarter immediately because they all read the same files.

---

## TWEET 5

An agent arriving on day one has the same institutional knowledge as one running for months. But only if the knowledge is encoded in the files. That single principle — the files are the memory — changed everything. It's the core of MESA.

---

## TWEET 6

The framework organizes agents in concentric rings radiating from a governance core. Intent layer sets direction. Orchestration translates intent into execution. Execution builds. Gates verify. Observation watches everything. Authority flows inward. Work flows outward.

---

## TWEET 7

The part that actually moves the needle: encoding your decision-making instincts into OWNER.yaml. Not "mission statement" stuff. How you actually think. With calibration percentages. "Honesty — 100%, bedrock." "Simplicity — 70%, says it but does features." That's operational intelligence.

---

## TWEET 8

And the learning system: don't codify every fix. Fix immediately, always. Log everything. When the same problem appears three times, it becomes permanent knowledge. One occurrence is an incident. Three is a pattern. The governance stays lean because it only grows when growth is earned.

---

## TWEET 9

Mechanical checkpoints, not behavioral ones. A checkpoint that depends on an agent remembering to use it isn't a checkpoint—it's a hope. Test every rule: Is it automatic? Does it hold under momentum? Does it have a trigger? Does it leave an artifact?

---

## TWEET 10

MESA is battle-tested across 30+ sessions. It emerged from pain, not a whiteboard. GigBoom runs on it right now. 11 agents. Real product. Real constraints. Every piece traces back to something that actually broke and a fix that became a pattern.
